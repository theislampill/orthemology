## 2. Formal modal analysis of O(x,r)=y

**What O represents.** The owner's gloss — "y is the state that *ought, would, or may properly* result" — packs three distinct modal operators into one: deontic (ought), dispositional/counterfactual (would), permissive (may). As written, O is therefore not one relation but a **task-indexed family**: a normative kernel (appropriateness) laid over a descriptive base (transition behavior). The four examples pull toward different members: protocol decoding is closest to *semantic interpretation over a descriptive transition* (the decoder would produce y, and y is the correct reading); sarf/nahw parsing is *normative-semantic* (y is the licensed analysis); da'wah diagnosis is *epistemic placement* (y locates x in a space of conditions given evidence); wiki organization is *teleological* (y serves the governing structure's purpose). One operator, four readings — this must be disambiguated per instance, or "appropriate" becomes an undefined container absorbing whatever each example needs.

**Output distinctions that must be kept apart** (the proposal currently lets "appropriate" cover all seven):

1. *actual* — what the episode in fact produced;
2. *admissible* — in the set licensed by r;
3. *expected* — what the realized mechanism would produce (dispositional);
4. *required* — admissible set is a singleton;
5. *possible* — reachable in the state space at all;
6. *optimal* — loss-minimizing among admissible outputs;
7. *correct / appropriately-routed* — satisfies the task's appropriateness relation *via a rule-licensed derivation that is stable across relevant perturbations*.

**Smallest formal signature.** Let an orthemic frame be

> **F = ⟨X, Y, R, A, N, E, Ag, ⊳⟩**

- X, Y: input/output state spaces (determinate, distinguishable states);
- R_T: a set of licensed partial derivations d: X ⇸ Y, **task-indexed** (the governing rules as procedures, not just as an extensional map);
- A_T ⊆ X × Y: **task-indexed** appropriateness (settable independently of what occurs — this is where "error is possible" lives; §6's ineliminable task-relativity now appears in the signature itself);
- N_T: X → P(X): a **task-indexed neighborhood (relevance) function** — for each input, the SET OF NEIGHBORING INPUTS relevant to reliability (corrected from an earlier `P(P(X))` typing, which mismatched every use of `x′ ∈ N(x)`; neighborhood-semantics machinery is not needed because no non-normal modal operator is used — an accessibility-style set suffices). Nothing requires N_T to range over metaphysically possible worlds; an engineered perturbation class suffices;
- E: optional evidence map (for epistemic instances), E(e) ⊆ facts available in episode e;
- Ag: actor index — **always populated**: a human, a validator, a pipeline, an institution. Mechanical executors are NON-CONSCIOUS ACTORS, not an empty slot (corrected; this matches the core formalization). Authority, where present, is a separate index from executor identity;
- L: a declared loss/decision standard (what "optimal relative to evidence" MEANS — absent a declared L, "optimal" is undefined);
- ⊳: a succession/handoff relation over episodes (for distributed and successor cases).

An episode is e = (x, d, y) with d ∈ R_T ∪ {unlicensed processes}.
**Partiality convention:** where d(x′) is undefined for a neighbor
x′ ∈ N_T(x), that neighbor counts as a route FAILURE at x′ (fail-closed),
not as a skipped case. Then:

- **successful:** (x, y) ∈ A_T;
- **incorrect:** (x, y) ∉ A_T;
- **correct-by-luck (Gettier-analogue):** (x, y) ∈ A_T but either d ∉ R_T, or d ∈ R_T and the failure rate of d over N_T(x) — a **declared measure and tolerance, not an undefined "many"** — exceeds the tolerance: actual correctness with cross-neighborhood failure of the route;
- **reasonable-but-mistaken:** d is optimal relative to E(e) among R_T **under the declared loss standard L**, yet (x, y) ∉ A_T because E(e) is incomplete — blameless error, distinct from luck;
- **mechanical:** Ag = the mechanical executor (non-conscious actor); d realized by physical dynamics engineered/selected to track R_T;
- **distributed:** e decomposes into sub-episodes {eᵢ} across actors/components with a composition operator; appropriateness of the whole requires interface conditions beyond the conjunction of part-appropriateness;
- **successor occurrences:** e ⊳ e′; correctness of e′ is conditional on the inherited state satisfying a stated invariant (otherwise e′ can be locally flawless and globally wrong).

**Assessment.** The full machinery (state space, neighborhoods, task/loss, constraints, actor indices, evidence relation, transition relation, cross-neighborhood reliability) is all genuinely needed to keep the seven output-notions apart — the proposal's informal schema underdetermines them. Crucially, **the signature is modal only in the logician's sense** (quantification over an alternatives-structure N). It can be fully interpreted inside an engineered or nomological state space. Nothing in the formalism forces, or even suggests, absolute metaphysical modality. That gap is load-bearing for everything at L4–L5.

## 3. Three modal theses

**(A) Weak: evaluating some episodes requires counterfactual reliability.**
- *Exact meaning:* some evaluative predicates ("non-lucky," "reliable," "trustworthy route") have truth conditions quantifying over N(x), not just the actual trace.
- *Strongest argument:* stopped-clock/Gettier. Actual match underdetermines evaluation; two episodes identical in actual trace can differ in luck-status. This is as secure as anything in the vicinity.
- *Counterexample:* none serious. One can refuse to use luck-discriminating vocabulary, but then one has changed the subject.
- *Stopped-clock/Gettier:* directly explained. *Deterministic decoding:* still applies — "the decoder works" vs "it emitted the right bits this once" differ. *Trivial rule-following:* the evaluation may be vacuous (degenerate N) but the thesis is untouched.
- *Defective orthing:* still orthing; (A) is silent on kind-membership.
- *Operation or evaluation?* **Evaluation only.** (A) is true, cheap, and carries no metaphysical weight.

**(B) Intermediate: non-defective orthing is partly defined by stability across relevant nearby states.**
- *Exact meaning:* the success-concept (the honorific "non-defective") is constitutively modal, in the way "reliable" is for reliabilists: to be a good orthing episode *just is*, in part, to be an episode whose route would succeed across N(x).
- *Strongest argument:* certification practice is not evidence-of but constitutive-of the concept: what we *mean* by a working decoder or sound grammatical analysis is perturbation-stable correctness; there is no perturbation-free residue of "working" left over.
- *Counterexample/pressure:* degenerate tasks (single-input lookup; one-shot ceremonial rule-applications) where N is empty or trivial — (B) holds vacuously, which shows it is not doing work everywhere.
- *Stopped-clock:* explains *why* lucky success is defective, not merely that it is. *Deterministic decoding:* satisfies (B) non-vacuously (noise neighborhoods). *Trivial rule-following:* vacuous satisfaction.
- *Defective orthing:* counts as orthing — (B) defines the success-grade, not the kind.
- *Operation or evaluation?* The **success-conditions of the operation-type**; still not the token's identity.

**(C) Strong: every orthing instance is essentially modal — the operation's identity depends on a structured possibility space.**
- *Exact meaning:* a token episode in a world lacking the alternatives-structure would not *be* an orthing episode (or not that one); modality enters the individuation of the token, not just its assessment.
- *Strongest argument (which the proposal needs but does not state):* rule-individuation. What makes a transition an application of rule r rather than a rival r′ agreeing on all actual cases is counterfactual behavior — the quus-style underdetermination from the rule-following literature, and cognate counterfactual accounts of what makes a physical process an implementation of a computation. Without the possibility space there is no fact that *this* rule governed, hence no fact that orthing (as opposed to a brute transition) occurred.
- *Counterexamples/pressure:* (i) the individuating modality can be dispositional/engineered — "essentially modal" then means "essentially dispositional," far short of metaphysical essence; (ii) a deflationist reply: tokens are brute physical transitions that we *classify* as orthing relative to a scheme, so the modality lives in the classification, not the token; (C) then mislocates it; (iii) Maudlin-style minimal-implementation worries: the counterfactual profile can be present in physically inert attachments, suggesting the profile is cheaper than "essence" implies.
- *Gettier relation:* **Gettier does not establish (C).** Gettier establishes (A)/(B) — evaluation and success are modal. The proposal's tie-in ("orthing is essentially modal" from route-failure across nearby possibilities) jumps from success-conditions to token-identity. The jump can perhaps be made, but only via the rule-individuation argument above, which is a different argument with different (contested) premises.
- *Defective orthing:* under (C), still orthing — identity is fixed by being governed, not by succeeding.
- *Operation or evaluation?* The operation — that is exactly why it is the expensive thesis.

**Verdict:** (A) established; (B) well-supported with a vacuousness caveat; (C) open — defensible only with an argument the proposal has not yet supplied, and even then the possibility space it needs is local/nomological unless further argued.

## 4. Substrate neutrality

**One architecture or loose analogy?** Proposed bounded criteria — individually near-necessary, jointly sufficient for an episode-type to count as orthing:

1. **Variety:** a determinate input type admitting more than one possible input.
2. **Norm/law gap:** a rule system R distinguishing licensed from unlicensed transitions, where unlicensed transitions remain *physically possible* (norm-governed, not merely law-governed).
3. **Independent standard:** an appropriateness relation A fixable independently of what actually occurs (error is possible).
4. **Assessability:** some possible assessor position from which episodes are classifiable correct/incorrect — the position need not be occupied, but must be definable.
5. **Non-triviality:** A excludes some physically possible outcomes.

Criterion 2 is the anti-trivialization clause: a rock rolling downhill satisfies a transition relation but nothing counts as its *erring*, so it does not orth. This blocks the collapse of orthing into "every causal transition."

Answers to the specific questions:

- *Representational input required?* Not by the architecture — a non-representational sorter with an external standard satisfies 1–5. But all four flagship examples are representational; **semantic orthing is a proper subtype**, and most of the proposal's philosophical weight (interpretation, meaning, Speech) rides on the subtype, not the genus. This should be stated in any writeup.
- *Several admissible alternatives?* Physically possible alternatives: yes (criterion 2). Normatively admissible alternatives: may be a singleton (required output).
- *Norm-governed vs law-governed?* Essential distinction; see criterion 2.
- *Error possible?* Criterial (3, 5).
- *Standards agent/designer-supplied?* In all four examples, yes — grammarians, scholars, editors, protocol committees. This is the architecture's exposed flank (§9.3).
- *Can natural processes orth without a designer?* The decisive test case is biological proofreading (transcription error-correction). If etiological/selected function suffices to fix A (a Millikan-style move), then nature orths without a designer but with a history that fixes norms — and orthing is genuinely substrate-neutral in the strong sense. If selected function does not suffice, orthing is designer-dependent and the generalization to "being as such" weakens sharply. The proposal needs to take a position; currently it has none.
- *Consciousness?* Irrelevant to the genus; relevant to the semantic/epistemic subtypes.
- *Malfunction:* **defective orthing** when the governing relation is still in force and the output violates A; **not-orthing** when the governing relation itself has lapsed (noise through a shredded decoder — no rule is any longer being applied, correctly or not).

**Verdict:** the four examples share a real functional architecture (each satisfies 1–5 non-trivially), so this is more than loose analogy. But functional co-instantiation is the unity of "engine" or "filter," not of a natural kind — real, useful, and ontologically lightweight. L2 is secured; nothing about L4 follows from it.

## 5. Modal grammar

**Disambiguation of "grammar."** At least nine readings are in play: (1) formal syntax; (2) transformation rules; (3) laws of nature; (4) semantic constraints; (5) social/epistemic norms; (6) a modal logic (frame + accessibility); (7) task-relative admissibility structure; (8) the space of metaphysical possibility itself; (9) divine knowledge of possibilities. The proposal's examples live at (1)–(2) and (7) (sarf, TCP/IP, repo schemas); its conclusion lives at (8)–(9). "Modal grammar" is therefore best read not as one literal structure but as a **family united by analogy, with the constitution argument attempting to show the family requires a limit case** that would be a transcendental condition. That is a coherent shape for an argument — but the analogical stretch from (2) to (8) must be earned, not assumed by the shared word "grammar."

**The constitution argument, reconstructed:**

- P1. Every created rule system r operates within a field of intelligibility F(r) — presupposed distinctness, possibility, consistency, appropriateness — that r does not itself create.
- P2. (Suppressed) Constitutive dependence is well-founded: chains of "operates within" terminate.
- P3. Nothing can be a contingent product inside the very possibility-space it constitutes.
- P4. (Suppressed) There is a single total field F* presupposed by all created systems jointly.
- C1. F* is not a contingent created product. (P3, P4)
- C2. There exists an uncreated ground of intelligibility. (C1, P2)

**Classification:** a transcendental argument with modal-cosmological shape — kin to Leibnizian arguments from eternal truths and to contemporary arguments from the necessity of logic to a necessary ground. Not the fallacy the owner rightly rejects ("orthing occurs, therefore orthing is necessary"); P3 is a genuine constitution principle, not a modal-scope slip.

**Self-reference/circularity risk:** moderate and structural. P3's "cannot" is itself a deliverance of the modal grammar whose status is at issue. For a transcendental argument this is tolerable — it argues *from within* the presupposition — but it caps the argument's dialectical force: it cannot compel someone who treats modal discourse as a useful fiction or as scheme-relative. It is an internal-coherence argument, not a neutral proof, and should be presented as such.

**Can rival positions satisfy the premises?**

| Position | P1 | P2 | P3 | P4 | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Infinite hierarchy of grammars | yes | **denies** | yes | denies | Blocked only by asserting well-foundedness of constitution — substantive, contested (metaphysical infinitism is a live position) |
| Brute modal facts (primitivism) | yes | yes | yes | yes | **Satisfies everything** — accepts C2 read as "some modal structure is uncreated," denies it is a *ground* in any explanatory or mental sense |
| Necessary abstracta (Platonism) | yes | yes | yes | yes | **Satisfies everything** up to C2; uncreated grammar = necessarily existing structure |
| Divine conceptualism | yes | yes | yes | yes | Satisfies everything and adds explanatory unity — at the price of the bootstrapping question below |

**What would distinguish them:** (a) whether normativity/aboutness requires a mind (the only argumentative route from C2 to conceptualism); (b) parsimony (favors primitivism); (c) explanatory depth (favors conceptualism or Platonism over brute facts); (d) the **bootstrapping objection**: does God's knowledge of possibilities presuppose a prior possibility-structure? The owner's own reductio, aimed at "all orthability is created," can be re-aimed at "all orthability is located in Knowledge" unless Knowledge is identified with the divine essence in the right way — which is precisely the school-dependent kalām terrain (§7). **The argument as given eliminates only "all modal structure is a contingent created artifact." It does not select among the three surviving positions.**

## 6. Orthability assessment

**Best analysis:** a relational, degreed, task-relative property — **orthable(D, T, R): domain D supports, relative to task T and repertoire R, the distinctions, stable transformations, and independently fixable appropriateness conditions required for rule-governed resolution.** Not a monadic property of reality; not a mere alias of intelligibility (intelligibility does not entail resolvability-by-a-given-repertoire); more than the bare conjunction of the five presuppositions, because the relation to a task and repertoire does inferential work.

- *Absence:* a domain of brute transitions with no perturbation-stable distinctions or no invariances exploitable by any repertoire — white-noise-like at every graining relative to T.
- *Degrees:* yes, and measurably — signal-to-noise ratio, identifiability, learnability, rule-compressibility are all local orthability measures. The concept generalizes existing technical notions ("decodability," "identifiability") rather than floating free of them.
- *Task-relativity:* ineliminable. A channel orthable for bitstreams is not orthable for aromas.
- *Engineerable?* Locally, yes — channel coding manufactures a decodable channel out of a noisy one. And here lies the strongest *honest* support for the owner's deeper claim: engineering local orthability visibly **presupposes background orthability** (physics stable enough to engineer against). Created-local orthability is real; the question is only whether the background regress terminates in something uncreated (§5) or is brute.
- *Prediction or restatement?* **Locally: earns its keep** — it licenses inferences from domain structure to feasibility of validators and detectors before building them, a distinct inferential role. **Globally ("orthability of being"): a limit-concept** — no observation could contrast an orthable with a non-orthable world from inside one, so it makes no differential predictions; it functions as a philosophical gloss, not a working concept.

**Verdict (Fable-revised):** `orthable(D, T, R)` **earns a distinct
inferential role analytically** — the relational signature generalizes
decodability/identifiability and is measurable in degrees, so it is not a
mere restatement. But per the terminology standard governing every coined
term here, *analytical distinctness is necessary, not sufficient, for
adoption*: the word is a **candidate**, not adopted, until the matched
Arm A/B/C terminology test (see `orthemic-terminology-evaluation-spec.md`)
shows it beats "decodability / identifiability / the domain is
resolvable-under-these-rules" on compression, coordination, or prediction.
The original "adopt … as a technical term" is downgraded accordingly — it
was adoption by analytical decree, which the review rules out. "Uncreated
orthability of being" remains quarantined as an L4 philosophical thesis,
never a working predicate.

## 8. Steelman

Strongest coherent version, every premise stated:

- **S1.** There exist norm-governed resolution systems: determinate inputs, licensed derivations, independently fixed appropriateness, possible error. (Empirical; secure.)
- **S2.** *Rule-individuation:* what makes a token transition an application of rule r, rather than a coextensive-on-actuals rival, is a set of counterfactual facts about behavior across non-actual inputs. (From the rule-following literature; contested but serious.)
- **S3.** Therefore every orthing token requires a determinate possibility-space with a normative partition — thesis (C). (From S1, S2.)
- **S4.** *Well-foundedness:* constitutive dependence has no infinite descending chains; presupposition structures terminate. (Metaphysical foundationalism about grounding.)
- **S5.** *Irreflexivity of constitution:* nothing constitutes its own preconditions; hence a transition from no-orthability to orthability cannot itself satisfy orthability-conditions it is supposed to originate. (Basis of the reductio; stronger and more defensible than the rejected "occurs therefore necessary.")
- **S6.** Therefore at least one locus of modal-normative structure is unconstituted — uncreated. (S3, S4, S5.)
- **S7.** *Mentalism about normativity:* error-as-error, appropriateness, and aboutness exist only relative to a perspective; unconstituted normative structure therefore resides in a necessary mind. (The most contestable philosophical premise.)
- **S8.** Therefore the uncreated locus is the knowledge of a necessarily existent mind. (S6, S7.)
- **S9.** *Revelational identification:* that mind is Allah; His Knowledge and Speech are uncreated attributes, per the owner's creed, with the Speech-doctrine specified per school. (Faith-relative.)
- **S10.** Therefore created languages, protocols, and minds are finite expressions of uncreated Knowledge/Speech, and every created act of orthing presupposes uncreated orthability. (S8, S9.)

Honesty notes on the steelman: S2 does the real work for (C) — the proposal must adopt it explicitly. S4 and S5 carry the reductio. S7 is the sole bridge from metaphysics to mind, and S9 is the sole bridge from mind to Allah; neither is supported by S1–S6, and the steelman is strongest when it says so.

## 9. Adversarial critique

Strongest objections, stated at full strength. Disagreements are preserved; no synthesis is forced.

1. **Descriptive/normative equivocation.** "Ought, would, or may properly" is three modalities under one operator. The substrate-neutrality case reads O descriptively for machines (the decoder *would*) and normatively for agents (the diagnostician *ought*). Unified only verbally.
2. **Local vs metaphysical modality — the deepest gap.** Every possibility-space in the examples is an engineered or nomological state space (bit-patterns, morphological paradigms, evidence states). The conclusion requires absolute metaphysical modality. No bridge premise is supplied anywhere in the proposal, and §2's formalism shows none is needed for the operational work — which is evidence the bridge is doing no work the examples support.
3. **Overgeneralization from engineered systems.** All four flagship instances have designers or norm-communities. Inferring "being as such is orthable" from a designer-selected sample is biased sampling; the one candidate natural case (biological proofreading) is unaddressed (§4).
4. **Functional equivalence ≠ ontological unity.** Multiple realization cuts against the proposal: "orthing" may be a kind like "filter" — perfectly real, predictively useful, and carrying zero weight about the deep structure of reality.
5. **Circularity in the constitution move.** P3's "cannot" is issued by the very modal grammar whose status is at issue; and the reductio's "intelligible AS creation" asks intelligible-*to whom*? If to us, the argument is anthropic; if intrinsically, it assumes reality must be intelligible — near the conclusion.
6. **Brute modal facts.** Primitivism grants "uncreated modal structure" at zero theological cost. The argument cannot distinguish its conclusion from primitivism without S7, which it has not defended.
7. **Necessary abstracta.** Platonism satisfies every non-revelational premise. The proposal's dismissal of "an autonomous Platonic machine standing above Allah" is a theological objection to Platonism, not an argument against it; within the proposal's own dialectic it is a stipulation.
8. **Task-relativity of appropriateness.** "Appropriate" is always indexed to a task and assessor-position (§2, §6). "Appropriateness of being" has no task index — a category stretch from a relational predicate to a monadic cosmic one.
9. **Conflation cluster.** Intelligibility (understandable), expressibility (encodable), determinacy (definite states), and language (symbol system) are run together in the "uncreated language is the ground of expressibility" formulation. A world could be determinate but unintelligible to any finite mind, or intelligible but not finitely expressible. Each identification needs its own argument.
10. **Knowledge vs Speech underdetermination.** Even granting divine grounding, whether modal space sits in ʿilm or kalām — and what "uncreated Speech" means — is school-dependent (§7.vii). The proposal's joint treatment quietly resolves a live intra-Islamic dispute.
11. **The reductio's step (2)–(3) is deniable.** A defender of "all orthability is created" can hold that creation ex nihilo is not a *process* with success-conditions, identity-across-transition, or an appropriateness relation — not an orthing-like transition at all. Then nothing requires the transition to "be intelligible as creation of orthability" from inside any prior orthability, and step (5) never engages. The reductio is valid against opponents who model creation as a rule-governed transition; it does not touch opponents who refuse that model. This is a standing disagreement, and it should be reported as one.

## 12. Proposed paper placement

**Verdict: separate companion paper** — with theses (A) and (B) retained in the main paper.

Reasons, none of which are "ambitious" or "theologically attractive":

1. **Evidential self-sufficiency of the main paper.** Everything the working paper needs lives at L1–L3(A/B): the operational schema, the §2 signature, the substrate criteria, and the counterfactual-robustness rationale for E5. These are independently defensible and should not inherit exposure to objections (9.2, 9.5–9.7, 9.11) that do not touch them. Integration would couple the paper's strongest material to its weakest premises.
2. **The metaphysical argument deserves full dress, not a cupboard.** The reductio and constitution argument are genuine arguments (better than the fallacy the owner rejects) whose assessment requires engaging infinitism, primitivism, Platonism, divine conceptualism, and the kalām attribute-disputes. A bounded speculative section would be simultaneously too long for the paper and too short for the argument — it could not state S1–S10, the rival-satisfaction table (§5), or the school-dependent flags (§7) responsibly.
3. **Audience separation.** The operational audience needs E5 and validator semantics; the philosophy-of-religion audience needs the transcendental argument with its alternatives. Neither is served by the other's apparatus.
4. **Confidence-gradient honesty.** The ladder runs high → high → mixed → low-moderate → faith-relative. A single integrated paper flattens that gradient toward its average; separation preserves it.
5. **Why not exclusion:** theses (A)/(B) and the underlying relational property (task-relative resolvability / counterfactual robustness, in ordinary words) are load-bearing for the main paper — they justify E5 and validator scope — and must stay. The coined term `orthable` is NOT load-bearing anywhere and remains a candidate. Excluding the L4–L5 material entirely would also waste a structurally interesting argument that merits its own examination — but on its own terms, under its own title, carrying its own burden.
