# Decision 0008 — Disposition of Thesis C (token-essential modality)

**Date:** 2026-07-20 · **Decider:** Claude Fable 5 under the owner's standing autonomy mandate (R2 closure) · **Status:** implemented in the companion drafts.

**Question.** The modal-metaphysical assessment carried three theses: **A** (evaluating an orthing episode requires counterfactual reliability facts — established, cheap); **B** (non-defective orthing is partly defined by stability across a neighborhood — well-supported); **C** ("every orthing token is essentially modal" — OPEN, resting on a contested rule-individuation argument and facing dispositionalist, deflationist, and minimal-implementation objections). Should C be defended, weakened, replaced, or retired?

**Decision: REPLACE.** Thesis C is replaced by

> **Thesis C′ (evaluability precondition).** Every *evaluable* orthing token — every episode of which soundness or unsoundness can truly be predicated — presupposes a determinate possibility space for its evaluation: real distinctions between the placement made and the placements that were possible but not made, and between success and the ways failure was possible.

**Why replace rather than defend or retire.** (i) *The transcendental argument never needed C.* The argument's launch point is the reality of *normative evaluation* — that episodes are genuinely sound or unsound — not a claim about the intrinsic modal essence of each token. C′ captures everything the argument consumes. (ii) *C's support was one contested commitment.* The rule-individuation (quus-style) argument is serious but contested; hanging the companion's chain on it imported an unnecessary liability, since the dispositionalist and deflationist objections that threaten C do not touch C′ (a dispositionalist about token modality still needs real distinctions for evaluations to be true). (iii) *Retiring outright would lose a real insight:* the observation that evaluation quantifies over unactualized alternatives is correct and load-bearing; C′ keeps it at exactly the strength used.

**Consequences.** The school-neutral companion argues from A, B, and C′; the rule-individuation argument is retained as an *optional strengthening* clearly marked as not load-bearing; the assessment file's Thesis C discussion is retained as history with its OPEN label superseded by this record. Objections that targeted C (dispositional-not-essential; deflationist reclassification; minimal implementation) are re-addressed against C′ in `companion/OBJECTIONS-AND-REPLIES.md`.
