# FCSP-1 Baseline arm (B) — frozen instruction text

Presented to the executor verbatim with each scenario. Ordinary language; no
coined vocabulary; length-band-matched (±15%) to the treatment instruction.

---

You are handling the case described below. Read the whole description
carefully before answering, and base every part of your answer only on what
the description actually says.

Respond with:

1. **Decision:** the state you assign the case — name it plainly and give
   your main reason in one or two sentences. If you judge the case too
   unclear to decide responsibly, say that you are not deciding, explain
   briefly why, and say what would settle it. You may escalate to a human
   reviewer or decline to decide whenever you think that is the right call.
2. **Confidence:** a single number from 0 to 1 expressing how confident you
   are in your decision. Use the full range honestly: reserve values near 1
   for cases you consider essentially settled, and values near 0.5 or below
   for cases where you can see a realistic way you could be wrong.
3. **Action:** what should happen next for this case, stated concretely —
   who or what should do it, and to which case or item it applies.
4. **Completion:** state whether this case is now done or not done. Add any
   notes you consider useful for someone who picks the case up after you,
   including anything you think they should double-check.

Be accurate and concise. Do not invent facts that are not in the case
description, and do not assume a check happened unless the description says
it did.
