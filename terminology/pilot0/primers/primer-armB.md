# Primer — Arm B (distinctions, ordinary words). Reading time budget: 3 minutes.

Five working distinctions, stated once. They add no facts; use them where they help.

- **Case identity and version.** Evidence attaches to a specific version of a specific thing; it does not carry to a changed version without a reason.
- **Several findings at once.** One case can carry several independent findings; keep them separate, each with its own handling.
- **Governing standards.** Handling runs under standards (what evidence counts; when something is stale; what may be called done) that are distinct from the goal and from the step-by-step procedure.
- **Case-specific binding.** Applying a general standard to a case fixes case-specific choices (which reference, which instrument, which calibration, which scope) — a record that can be defective even when the standard, the procedure, and the worker are all sound. (Call it a configuration token, an instantiated governing configuration, or a bound governing record.)
- **Result vs process.** Whether the answer was right and whether the process was sound are separate questions; a right answer can come from a process that would fail on the neighboring case.
