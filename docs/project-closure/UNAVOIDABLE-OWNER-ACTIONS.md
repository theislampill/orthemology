# Unavoidable owner-only actions

> **R3 revision (2026-07-20).** The R2 version of this list contained an item ("approve classical-edition sources") that was too broad: ordinary verification of publicly accessible primary and secondary sources is research work, performed autonomously in R3 — it is owner-blocked only where a genuinely necessary source is inaccessible without purchase or private access **after a documented search**. The list below is the narrow, current one.

The complete list of what only the owner can do. Nothing else in the lane is owner-blocked; no ordinary editorial or research judgment appears here.

1. **Choose a legal license** (none exists; default copyright applies until then).
2. **Supply/approve bibliographic identity** — author name, affiliation, ORCID — enabling CITATION.cff and any numbered release.
3. **Fund and execute the empirical program** — pinned-model runs (spend), ≥3 human raters (participants/responsibility), and the confirmatory freeze decision; every packet is READY TO RUN, NOT RUN.
4. **Publish externally** — preprint server, journal, or DOI archive under the owner's identity.
5. **Acquire genuinely inaccessible paid sources** — only where the R3 sourcing audit documents that no adequate public scan, edition, library copy, quotation-in-context, or reputable secondary verification is available (each such source is itemized in `docs/project-closure/r3/R3-SOURCING-AUDIT.md`).
6. **Decide on casebook publication** (privacy/rights call; the public corpus does not depend on it).
