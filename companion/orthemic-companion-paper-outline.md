## 0. Working title and abstract sketch

- **Working title:** *Rule-Governed Resolution and the Uncreated Ground of
  Intelligibility: A Transcendental Argument from Orthing*
- **Subtitle option:** *An Internal-Coherence Argument, Not a Neutral Proof*
- **Abstract sketch (to be drafted, ~200 words):**
  - Start from an operationally attested phenomenon: norm-governed
    resolution of determinate inputs to appropriate outputs ("orthing"),
    instantiated in grammar parsing, epistemic diagnosis, schema
    maintenance, protocol decoding (imported from the main paper, L1–L2).
  - Argue that evaluating such episodes is counterfactual (thesis A,
    established) and that their success-grade is constitutively modal
    (thesis B, well-supported).
  - Advance the contested step: token identity is modal (thesis C), via a
    rule-individuation argument.
  - Run the constitution argument (S1–S10): not all modal-normative
    structure can be a contingent created artifact; some locus is uncreated.
  - **Framing commitment, stated in the abstract itself:** this is a
    transcendental, internal-coherence argument that argues from within
    modal discourse (§5); it cannot compel the modal fictionalist or
    scheme-relativist and is not presented as a neutral proof.
  - Close by candidly reporting what survives elimination (primitivism,
    Platonism, divine conceptualism, and — absent well-foundedness —
    infinitism) and that theological identification is faith-relative and
    school-dependent.

## 1. Section-by-section skeleton

### §I. The operational base (imported by reference)

- Import L1 (specific systems exist and resolve under explicit rules) and
  L2 (one substrate-neutral functional architecture, per the five §4
  criteria: variety; norm/law gap; independent standard; assessability;
  non-triviality) **by reference to the main paper** — no re-derivation.
- The `F = ⟨X, Y, R, A, N, E, Ag, ⊳⟩` signature (§2) summarized in one
  subsection; note that the signature is modal only in the logician's
  sense and is fully interpretable inside engineered/nomological state
  spaces.
- **Explicit statement, verbatim commitment:** *no engineering evidence
  supports anything beyond L2.* Evidence for level n never auto-counts for
  level n+1 (§1 ladder rule). The systems' behavior underwrites no
  metaphysical or theological claim in this paper.
- Note the semantic-subtype point (§4): all four flagship examples are
  representational; the paper's weight rides on the subtype, not the genus.

### §II. The modal theses

- **Thesis A** (evaluation requires counterfactual reliability): established
  via Gettier/stopped-clock; cheap; carries no metaphysical weight (§3).
- **Thesis B** (non-defective orthing is partly defined by stability across
  N(x)): well-supported; state the vacuousness caveat (degenerate tasks).
- **Thesis C** (every orthing token is essentially modal): **OPEN.** This
  section must:
  - state that Gettier does NOT deliver C (§3, §9 objection framing);
  - supply the **rule-individuation argument (S2)** — quus-style
    underdetermination; counterfactual implementation accounts — as the
    paper's **first load-bearing philosophical commitment**, adopted
    explicitly, not gestured at;
  - present the three standing pressures on C: dispositional-not-essential
    modality; deflationist classification reply; minimal-implementation
    ("Maudlin-style") worries — pending sourcing backlog items 4–5.
- Honest note: even if C is secured, the possibility space obtained is
  local/nomological unless the bridge premise of §III is added.

### §III. The constitution argument (every premise explicit)

- Reconstruction P1–P4 → C1–C2 (§5) and steelman S1–S10 (§8), each premise
  labeled with type and paired with its strongest objection:
  - **P1 / S1** — created rule systems presuppose a field of intelligibility
    they do not create; norm-governed systems exist. *Type:* empirical (S1)
    / near-analytic on the weak reading (P1). *Strongest objection:*
    deflationism — "preconditions" are our descriptions (§7.i); on the
    strong reading P1 is no longer analytic.
  - **S2** — rule-individuation by counterfactual facts. *Type:*
    metaphysical (philosophy of mind/computation; contested but serious).
    *Strongest objection:* the individuating modality is dispositional/
    engineered; the deflationist locates it in the classification (§3.C).
  - **S3** — every orthing token requires a determinate possibility space
    (thesis C). *Type:* derived (S1, S2). *Strongest objection:* inherits
    S2's; modality shown is local/nomological (§9.2).
  - **P2 / S4** — well-foundedness of constitutive dependence. *Type:*
    metaphysical (grounding foundationalism). *Strongest objection:*
    metaphysical infinitism is a live position; asserted, not defended
    (§11.4).
  - **P3 / S5** — nothing constitutes its own preconditions; irreflexivity
    of constitution. *Type:* metaphysical (constitution principle — not the
    rejected modal-scope fallacy). *Strongest objection:* circularity —
    P3's "cannot" is issued by the very modal grammar at issue (§9.5); and
    the reductio's step (2)–(3) is deniable if creation ex nihilo is not an
    orthing-like transition (§9.11).
  - **P4** — single total field F* presupposed by all created systems
    jointly. *Type:* metaphysical (totality/uniqueness). *Strongest
    objection:* nothing argues for singularity; plural or overlapping local
    fields (§7.iv, §11.5).
  - **S6 / C1–C2** — at least one locus of modal-normative structure is
    uncreated. *Type:* derived (S3, S4, S5). *Strongest objection:*
    primitivists and Platonists accept it at zero theological cost (§9.6–7).
  - **Unlabeled but required — the modality bridge:** from engineered/
    nomological possibility spaces to absolute metaphysical modality.
    *Type:* metaphysical. *Strongest objection:* nowhere argued; the §2
    formalism shows the operational work needs no such bridge (§9.2,
    §11.3). Must appear as an explicit premise, not an ambient assumption.
  - **S7** — mentalism about normativity (error-as-error requires a
    perspective; the unconstituted normative structure resides in a
    necessary mind). *Type:* metaphysical (the most contestable premise).
    *Strongest objection:* Platonism/primitivism deny minds are needed for
    normativity (§9.7); the proposal's anti-Platonism is theological, not
    argumentative.
  - **S8** — the uncreated locus is the knowledge of a necessarily existent
    mind. *Type:* derived (S6, S7).
  - **S9** — that mind is Allah; Knowledge and Speech uncreated per the
    owner's creed, Speech-doctrine per school. *Type:* revelational
    (faith-relative; school-dependent). *Strongest objection:*
    underdetermination — nothing in S1–S8 selects a specific revelation
    (§7.vi); ʿilm-vs-kalām location is disputed (§9.10).
  - **S10** — created languages/protocols/minds as finite expressions of
    uncreated Knowledge/Speech. *Type:* derived (S8, S9) + revelational.
    *Strongest objection:* monadic "orthability of being" strains the
    relational, task-indexed signature (§9.8); the intelligibility/
    expressibility/determinacy/language conflation cluster (§9.9).
- Classification subsection: transcendental argument with modal-
  cosmological shape (§5); internal-coherence status stated here again.

  the section must say so rather than resolve it.
