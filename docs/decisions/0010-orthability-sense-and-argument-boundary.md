# Decision 0010 — Orthability senses and the argument boundary

**Date:** 2026-07-20 · **Authority:** R3 owner authorization · **Status:** adopted · **Interacts with:** 0008 (C′), 0009 (typing); reopens nothing.

## Problem

The R2 school-neutral companion defined orthability as "always relative to the resolving practice's standards" while arguing that orthability is antecedent to every created practice. Unbridged, that is an equivocation: a created practice cannot both constitute the relativity base and stand downstream of the property said to precede all created practice.

## Decision

### 1. Three senses, machine-distinguishable

- **Orthability-L (local, analysis-relative aptness):** `Orthable(m; A)` — occurrence `m` is apt for determinate, correct/incorrect resolution under a declared **or possible** analysis `A`.
- **Orthability-O (objective conditions of evaluability):** the identity, difference, modal possibility, truth/error, fittingness, lawful-transition, and success/failure conditions that make any adequate analysis — and any evaluable orthing — possible. Not practice-relative.
- **Orthability-R (created representation/application):** a finite practice's observation, evidence, placement, rule, and execution — the created machinery that instantiates and applies L-facts within O-conditions.

Every occurrence of "orthability" in current companion prose must be readable under exactly one sense; the transcendental argument's target is **O**; the operational framework's predicate is **L**; practices live at **R**.

### 2. The L→O bridge is stated, never smuggled

The chain's step 2 states the bridge explicitly: from the *truth* of evaluations of actual episodes (not their mere occurrence) to the objective conditions those truths presuppose. Moving from L to O by relabeling the practice-relative predicate as a global object is forbidden; the validator's sense check enforces the presence of the sense apparatus wherever the companion uses the word intensively.

### 3. Argument-map discipline

`companion/ARGUMENT-MAP-ORTHABILITY.md` classifies every step (analytic / transcendental / explanatory / anti-primitivist / anti-Platonist / teleological / causal-adequacy / metaphysical / revelational / school-internal), states every extra premise, and separates: explanatory metalanguage dependence · ontological priority · necessity · personality/intellect · Will · Power · Wisdom · capacity for Speech · actual divine Speech and its revealed modality. The self-assembly reductio remains strong against constructive accounts that generate all determinacy through an intelligible norm-governed transition, and is **not** represented as defeating without remainder: brute modal primitivism, Platonism, structural realism, the deflationary metalanguage reply, or every emergence account whatsoever (the companion's §5.3 adjudication is retained and now sense-annotated).

### 4. No entity reification

Orthability-O is a family of *conditions*, not an entity beside concrete reality; the anti-Platonist step argues explanatory superiority, not the existence of a new object called "Orthability." (Decision 0009 §6 applies.)

## Consequences

Companion vocabulary note rewritten; §4.2 steps 2–3 restated with the explicit bridge; §5 reductio quantified over sense O; objections ledger gains the equivocation objection (G1) with its reply; sense check added to `validate_type_token_semantics.py`.
