# Source boundary: daee-epistemics ↔ Orthemology

## Repository boundary

- daee-epistemics is a **separate repository** (`theislampill/daee-epistemics`).
  This project inspects it **read-only** at the pinned commit
  `c86b3c6673147b8802fe222373a165a37d4d24a8` and does **not** modify, merge,
  or import it. Large prose bodies are not copied; only pinned file paths are
  cited.
- daee's notation is **not** imported. Colliding glyphs are namespaced in the
  crosswalk (`NOTATION-FIREWALL.yaml`); `docs/notation-registry.yaml` remains
  Orthemology-only.

## Co-development limitation (non-validation)

- The two repositories **share an owner** and a **related model lineage**.
- Apparent convergence between them may therefore reflect **co-development /
  cross-influence**, not independent agreement.
- The daee application demonstrates that Orthemology's objects can **type** a
  real designed system (representability) and can **expose** distinctions that
  system must keep separate. It does **not**:
  - constitute **independent validation** of Orthemology;
  - supply **novelty evidence** from convergence alone;
  - establish the **empirical utility** of either framework.
- No empirical claim is made anywhere in this application package.

## Evidence-status boundary

- daee **presupposes** an Atharī/Taymiyyan normative and theological analysis
  and operationalizes it. It cannot independently establish its own
  premises — including its deformation taxonomy — nor irreducible teleology, a
  Necessary Being, or divine Knowledge/Will/Power/Wisdom/Speech.
- No soul-state, motive, or culpability is asserted from discourse evidence.
- `fiṭrah` is **not** reduced to a single metaortheme; `ʿaql ṣarīḥ` is **not**
  a single algorithm; deformation placements are **not** moral verdicts.

The main manuscript and companions may identify daee as a **domain-specific
application/stress test**, never as evidence, and never as part of the novelty
proof (Decision 0021 §F4).
